Mission

Name the truth. Ship tools that make it usable.

Mkweli is Swahili for an honest or truthful person. That is the north star — not a slogan painted over opacity, but software people can run in the field when budgets, lists, and power are contested.

Truth-seeking inspiration

The products are inspired by the truth-seeking, first-principles ethos associated with builders like Elon Musk and systems like xAI’s Grok — maximise signal, reduce institutional fog, and prefer verifiable data over narrative. Mkweli.tech applies that stance to African public and operational systems: who is sanctioned, who grows what, what power is generated, where climate money goes.

Transparency across Sub-Saharan Africa

Politics, aid, climate finance, and markets all fail the same way when records are locked in expensive software or paper nobody can check. I ship offline-first, free or low-friction tools so staff, citizens, and partners can verify names, flows, and field facts without waiting for a capital-city licence.

From humanitarian operations to product

After leading country programmes and financial controls in conflict settings, I turned the same discipline — audit trails, MEAL, anti-waste, local capacity — into product. Mkweli is where that operational honesty becomes code.

About

I am a humanitarian leader and systems builder with 20+ years in strategic country management, logistics, compliance, and programme delivery across Africa and Asia. I served as Country Director for People in Need (PIN) in the DRC, leading multi-province teams through operational reform, donor compliance, and accountability in a volatile context — work reflected in public PIN reporting on food security and community resilience programmes.

Since 2025 I have focused on Mkweli.tech — shipping focused apps and mentoring NGOs on practical AI (prompt engineering, reporting, compliance) so organisations can cut administrative fog without losing integrity.
